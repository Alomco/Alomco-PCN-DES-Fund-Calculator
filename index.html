Additional Information and Resources

About DES Funding

The Network Contract Directed Enhanced Service (DES) provides funding to Primary Care Networks (PCNs) to support the delivery of services to patients. This funding is designed to help PCNs develop and maintain integrated services, improve access, and enhance care quality.

Understanding List Sizes

Raw List Size: The actual number of patients registered with practices in a PCN without any modifications.

Adjusted List Size: The modified raw count accounting for practical factors such as PCN membership changes. This value is used for calculating most funding elements.

Weighted List Size: A transformation of the raw count to reflect the varying healthcare needs of different demographic groups, primarily used for ARRS funding allocation.

Historical Adjustment Factor: The percentage difference between raw and adjusted list sizes based on historical data. This factor is used to estimate the adjusted list size when only raw list size is available. For example, if a PCN's adjusted list size is typically 5% higher than its raw list size, the historical adjustment factor would be 5%.

Historical Weighting Factor: The percentage difference between raw and weighted list sizes based on historical data. This factor is used to estimate the weighted list size when only raw list size is available. For example, if a PCN's weighted list size is typically 10% higher than its raw list size, the historical weighting factor would be 10%.

Funding Components

Core PCN Funding: £2.999 per patient, calculated as (£2.266 × raw list size + £0.733 × adjusted population).

Extended Access: Calculated as £8.427 × adjusted population.

Care Home Premium: £130.253 per bed per year.

PCN Capacity and Access Support (CAIP): Calculated as £3.208 × adjusted population. When the "Use CAIP Local Rate" option is selected, this rate is increased by 15% to £3.689 per patient to reflect the higher funding available through local commissioning arrangements.

ARRS Funding: ARRS Funding: Calculated as £26.631 per weighted patient, based on the PCN’s weighted population. The formula used is: £26.631 × PCN weighted population. For example, a PCN with a weighted population of 50,000 would receive: 50,000 × £26.631 = £1,331,550..

IIF Funding: Investment and Impact Fund, a performance-based incentive scheme. Calculated as £198 per point achieved, with a maximum of 58 points available. The total potential IIF funding is £11,484 (58 points × £198).

Participation Fund: Funding that goes directly to practices for participation in the PCN DES. Calculated as £1.761 × weighted population. This funding is paid directly to practices rather than to the PCN.

Calculation Methods

This calculator uses the official formulas from the NHS England Network Contract DES Specification 2025/26. When adjusted or weighted list sizes are missing, they are estimated based on:

  • PCN-specific historical data from 2024/25 (where available).
  • National average adjustment factors (as a fallback).
  • User-provided custom adjustment factors.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this calculator?
This calculator employs the official funding rates and formulas from the NHS England Network Contract DES Specification 2025/26. Its accuracy depends on the quality of the input data. For best results, use the official list sizes provided by NHS England.

Why are my adjusted and weighted list sizes different from the raw list size?
Adjusted list sizes reflect modifications due to PCN membership changes and other practical factors, while weighted list sizes account for differing healthcare needs. Both metrics are calculated using complex formulas by NHS England and typically result in higher values than the raw list sizes.

How is ARRS funding calculated?
ARRS funding is distributed based on a PCN's weighted population share from a total of £1.7bn for 2025/26. Although final allocations are determined by NHS England, this calculator provides an estimate based on the weighted list size.
